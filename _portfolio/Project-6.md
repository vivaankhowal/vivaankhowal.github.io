## About
As part of my FIRST Robotics Team, I was tasked with designing a cart to transport our robot around competition easily plus carrying a couple other useful items such as toolboxes, a programming laptop, some extra parts, an extra battery and our team's flag. After about 4 months of off and on work alongside a few other team members designing and building, this is what we came up with.

There was one catch: we didn't have a working CNC machine at the time so I couldn't design any custom metal brackets or mounting for this project. It was tricky trying to find a design that fit all those needs plus didn't use any custom metal parts, but I think it turned out pretty well.

## Hardware
The main baseplate of the cart is made from 3/4" MDF as well as the station at the top. The frame is made from a combination of aluminum extrusions and 2x1s mixed with a different assortment of 1/4 20 bolts and some 10-32 screws + nuts.
<p></p>
<img src='/images/Robot Cart/IMG_0177.JPG' width='500' height='auto'>
<p></p>
The cart runs on 4 wheels which can all lock allowing for both omnidirectional movement and the ability to stay rigid even with the robot on top of it. As an added bonus, it lets us work underneath the robot just like working under a jacked-up car.
<p></p>
<img src='/images/Robot Cart/IMG_0189.JPG' width='500' height='auto'>
<p></p>
The main part of the cart can store 2 big toolboxes plus some smaller boxes, batteries or anything else you need to put underneath. The 2 3D printed hooks on the side are to hold our team flag, so the announcer can wave it at the beginning of a competition round. There are 2 solid aluminum 2x1s on the top to hold the actual robot.
